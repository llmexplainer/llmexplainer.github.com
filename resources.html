What is AI appropriate for?

It's important to understand what AI tools can do, but the question often remains: what should should they do? In Draw the Line by Fred Wordie, reflect on and decide where you personally draw the line on what human tasks should be delegated to computers. In semi-satirical Why Don't You Ask ChatGPT? question over-reliance on AI agents and explore alternatives.

Environmental, labor, and local costs

While AI technologies may seem to exist only on our mobile or computer devices, the underlying computational processes require physical space and resources. Data centers that host computing hardware required to train AI often come with large financial and environmental costs, such as a large use of energy and land often felt by local communities where the centers are based. Beyond the facade of automation, post-training and finetuning of AI requires large-scale human labour, which can include the exploitation of workers around the world.

  • AI has an environmental problem. Here’s what the world can do about that by the UN Environment Programme
  • OpenAI Used Kenyan Workers on Less Than $2 Per Hour to Make ChatGPT Less Toxic by Billy Perrigo [Time]
  • The hidden Kenyan workers training China’s AI models by Damilare Dosunmu and Tessie Waithira [Rest of World]
